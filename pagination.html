Cubing

The Rubik's Cube is a 3D combination puzzle invented in 1974[2][3] by Hungarian sculptor and professor of architecture Ern≈ë Rubik. Originally called the Magic Cube,[4] the puzzle was licensed by Rubik to be sold by Pentangle Puzzles in the UK in 1978,[5] and then by Ideal Toy Corp in 1980[6] via businessman Tibor Laczi and Seven Towns founder Tom Kremer.[7] The cube was released internationally in 1980 and became one of the most recognised icons in popular culture. It won the 1980 German Game of the Year special award for Best Puzzle. As of January 2024, around 500 million cubes had been sold worldwide,[8][9][10] making it the world's bestselling puzzle game[11][12] and bestselling toy.[13] The Rubik's Cube was inducted into the US National Toy Hall of Fame in 2014.[14] On the original, classic Rubik's Cube, each of the six faces was covered by nine stickers, with each face in one of six solid colours: white, red, blue, orange, green and yellow. Some later versions of the cube have been updated to use coloured plastic panels instead. Since 1988, the arrangement of colours has been standardised, with white opposite yellow, blue opposite green and orange opposite red, and with the red, white and blue arranged clockwise, in that order.[15] On early cubes, the position of the colours varied from cube to cube.[16] A salaryman trying to solve a Rubik's Cube in an izakaya after work in Japan, 2008 An internal pivot mechanism enables each layer to turn independently, thus mixing up the colours. For the puzzle to be solved, each face must be returned to having only one colour. The cube has inspired other designers to create a number of similar puzzles with various numbers of sides, dimensions and mechanisms. Although the Rubik's Cube reached the height of its mainstream popularity in the 1980s, it is still widely known and used. Many speedcubers continue to practise it and similar puzzles, competing for the fastest times in various categories. Since 2003, the World Cube Association (WCA), the international governing body of the Rubik's Cube, has organised competitions worldwide and has recognised world records.

Watching TV

Watching TV is a popular pastime that involves viewing television programs on a screen. It can be a source of entertainment, news, and educational content. Many people enjoy watching shows, movies, and documentaries for relaxation or to stay informed. Television has evolved significantly since its invention in the early 20th century. Modern TVs offer high-definition displays, smart features, and access to streaming services, providing viewers with a wide range of options. With the rise of on-demand content, viewers can now choose what to watch and when to watch it, making TV viewing more personalized than ever before. TV shows often reflect cultural trends and societal issues, influencing public opinion and sparking conversations. Genres range from drama and comedy to reality TV and news programs, catering to diverse audiences. However, excessive TV watching has been linked to sedentary lifestyles and health issues, prompting recommendations for balanced viewing habits. Overall, watching TV remains a popular and influential medium in contemporary society, shaping culture and providing entertainment to millions worldwide.

Reading Books

Reading books is a beloved hobby that allows individuals to explore different worlds, ideas, and perspectives. It can be both a form of entertainment and a way to gain knowledge. Readers often enjoy various genres, from fiction and fantasy to non-fiction and biographies. The act of reading has numerous benefits, including improved cognitive function, enhanced vocabulary, and increased empathy. It can also serve as a form of escapism, providing a mental break from the stresses of daily life. With the advent of digital technology, reading has become more accessible through e-books and audiobooks, allowing people to enjoy literature on the go. Book clubs and literary communities have also flourished, fostering discussions and connections among readers. Overall, reading books remains a cherished activity that enriches the mind and soul.
